What this is

The City Record is NYC's official daily journal: every agency must publish its procurement solicitations, award notices, public hearings, and property dispositions there (City Charter §1066). This tool reads that same data live from NYC Open Data — dataset City Record Online (dg92-zbpx), the structured form of the printed paper — and re-links notices that share a Procurement Identification Number (PIN).

Why "how to respond" matters: NYC runs bids through PASSPort (the MOCS vendor portal). The City Record is the open legal record of every opportunity ≥ $100K — but it buries the deadline and contact. This tool surfaces them.

Honest caveats (the data is messy)

• RFPs rarely list a dollar value — that's why "Min award $" applies to the Awards view; the money lives there.

• Junk & blanket PINs. Some notices carry placeholder PINs that can't be linked; some PINs bundle many unrelated awards. The tool flags both.

• Dollar outliers. Raw contract_amount contains erroneous mega-values; agency totals exclude rows above $5B. Confirm any figure against the official City Record and Checkbook NYC.
